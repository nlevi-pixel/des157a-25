This digital bookshelf exhibits my personal items that have generations of history behind their craft. You'll find that they are hidden behind stickers of mass produced replacements that I, and the rest of society, encounter today.

Volkswagen

1980

Admittedly, I have never owned a Volkswagen from 1980. Nevertheless, when I first encountered this car I couldn't help but reflect on the craftsmanship of cars before out time. The intricute paint job and sharp curves of the overall shape are wonderful characteristics, which I find sadly have been lost in time

Porcelein Cameo

1930

This cameo, which I wear everyday and have been for the past 6 years, is the most valuable item I own. In addition to it's properties made up of porceilein and gold, it's value lies as a family heirloom for the past 3 generations. Today, cameos like this are mass produced by use of cheap plastic and resin. How sad.

Sichuan Drawer

1920

I came across this jewelry box with my mother at an antique shop, in which the cashier was able to share a short history of the box and it's journey overseas. Again the jewlery boxes exemplify the loss of cratsmanship and passion that once commonly went into the creation of material items.

Wooden Vanity

1980

Another item I received from my childhood, although relatively newer to my collection, it was also purchased by mother in her 30s. I absolutely adore the design and authenticity of this vanity mirror, especiallly with the abundunce of steel, lifeless vanities that are majorly produced today.
